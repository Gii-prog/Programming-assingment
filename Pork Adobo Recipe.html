Considered by some as the pride of Filipino cuisine, Pork Adobo is definitely a favorite among many around the world. For this, we cook pork slices in a perfect mix of soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic to make this savory and sour viand. And I’ve got to say, it’s definitely a great way to introduce the world to what Pinoy cooks can make! But actually, there are other renditions of adobo.

Ingredients:

  • 2kg pork belly, any cut
  • minced garlic
  • onion (optional)
  • bay leaves
  • salt and pepper
  • 6-8 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1-2 cups water
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar (optional)

Instructions:

In a pan heat oil and sauté minced garlic until fragrant. Add onion and cook until translucent. Add the pork belly and cook until lightly browned. Pour in soy sauce, vinegar, and water. Add bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then simmer until pork is tender. Adjust seasoning and add sugar if desired. Serve hot with rice.
